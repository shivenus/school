What are the differences between a stack and a queue?

stack
it's a LIFO structure, a node can be pushed and pulled from only at the top.

queue.
a FIFO structure, nodes are added at the end of this type of linked list, and removed from the beginning of it.

book response 
A queue has pointers to both its head and its tail so that nodes may be inserted at the tail and deleted from the head.
A stack has a single pointer to the top of the stack where both insertion and deletion of nodes is performed.
